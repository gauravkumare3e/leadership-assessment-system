How to use this report?

The objective of this report is to facilitate your development. The report is designed to enable you to:

  • Create awareness of your perceived strengths and areas of development.
  • Create development plans on identied focus areas for enhancing leadership skills.

It is important to focus on the elements that are critical for leadership and prioritize your development based on leveraging areas that are strengths and closing the gaps between current and ideal performance levels.

It is also critical that you spend time with your manager discussing the report and then evaluating the expectations.

It is important to take note of the positive aspects of the results. Once recognized, these strengths can be used to develop your full potential.

Understanding the graphs:

The graphs can be used to see if your perception of self is in line with how your supervisor/ line manager sees you. Your results can fall into any one of the following categories:

  • Open / Known Strengths: Elements that are recognized/ perceived as strengths by self and your supervisor i.e. rating by self and others is similar. This implies that you recognize this element as strength and so do your stakeholders.
  • Hidden / Unknown strengths: Elements that are known as strengths by others but not recognized as strengths by self i.e. rating by others is significantly higher than that by self. This implies that you may be unaware of some strength you demonstrate.
  • Known Areas of Development: Elements that are recognized/ perceived as areas of development by self and your supervisor i.e. rating by self and others is similar on recognizing some areas as being critical focus areas for development. This implies that you have already identified this element as being a development area for yourself.
  • Blind Spots / Unknown Areas of Development: Elements that are recognized as areas of development by your supervisor but not by self i.e. rating by others is significantly lower than that by self.
